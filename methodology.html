Methodology & Confidence
Transparency · Confidence · Limitations
How Vitics generates
intelligence
Every insight on the Vitics dashboard has a methodology behind it. This page explains exactly how each metric is calculated, what data it draws from, how confident we are, and what its limitations are. We believe transparency in method is as important as the insight itself.
Confidence system
How we signal confidence

Every Vitics insight carries a confidence indicator — a visual signal showing how reliable that specific metric is given its data source, sample size, and analytical method. Three levels are used:

High confidence
Large sample · Official API · Validated model
Moderate confidence
Adequate sample · Modelled estimate · Some uncertainty
Indicative
Small sample · Proxy measure · Treat as directional only
Data pipeline
How data flows through Vitics
1
Collection via official APIs
Publicly available content is collected through official platform APIs (YouTube Data API v3, Reddit PRAW, NewsAPI). No unauthorised scraping is used. Collection is keyword and topic-scoped to Nigerian energy discourse.
2
Anonymisation & PII removal
All usernames, profile references, and identifying metadata are stripped before any storage or processing. Only anonymised text content and engagement metadata proceed to the next stage.
3
AI analysis via Claude (Anthropic API)
Anonymised content is processed by Claude to extract: sentiment polarity, topic clusters, product mentions, geographic references, SDG-relevant themes, and fossil vs clean energy signals. Unlike black-box models, Claude provides reasoning alongside scores.
4
Aggregation & structured data integration
AI-extracted signals are aggregated across all collected content. Structured reference data from IEA, GOGLA, REA, and FMENV is integrated to contextualise consumer signals within verified emissions and market baselines.
5
Insight generation & confidence scoring
Final insights are generated with confidence levels assigned based on sample size, source quality, and model certainty. All insights are labelled with their data basis before display on the dashboard.
Metric methodologies
How each dashboard metric is calculated
Conversations tracked
High
YouTube APIForumsReddit
Total count of publicly available posts, comments, videos, and forum threads matching Nigerian energy keywords collected in the current 30-day window. Counted at the point of API retrieval before anonymisation.
Limitations
Reflects API-accessible content only — not all public content is indexed
Skews toward English and Nigerian Pidgin; Hausa/Yoruba/Igbo content may be undercounted
Overall sentiment score
Moderate
Claude AIAll sources
Percentage of analysed conversations classified as positive, neutral, or negative toward decentralised energy adoption. Scored by Claude on a per-conversation basis then aggregated. Positive = favourable views on solar, mini-grid, clean energy. Negative = complaints, affordability concerns, reliability failures.
Limitations
AI sentiment classification carries inherent uncertainty (~85% accuracy on Nigerian energy vocabulary)
Sarcasm, irony, and code-switching in Nigerian discourse may be misclassified
Does not capture offline sentiment from non-digital populations
CO₂ avoided estimate
Indicative
IEA dataGOGLAModel
Estimated metric tonnes of CO₂ equivalent avoided through consumer adoption of decentralised renewable energy in Nigeria. Calculated using IPCC/IEA emission factors for displaced diesel and kerosene, cross-referenced against GOGLA off-grid solar market data and Vitics-derived adoption proxy signals. This is a modelled estimate, not a metered measurement.
Limitations
Adoption rates are proxy-estimated from conversation signals, not verified sales data
Emission factors are global averages — Nigeria-specific factors may differ
Treat as directional indicator, not reportable emissions accounting
SDG alignment scores
Moderate
Claude AIUN SDG taxonomy
Percentage of energy conversations that contain themes aligned with each tracked SDG target. Claude classifies each conversation against a Nigeria-specific SDG taxonomy. SDG 7 (energy access), SDG 5 (gender), SDG 8 (economic growth), SDG 10 (inequality), SDG 13 (climate). Score = proportion of relevant conversations showing positive alignment signals.
Limitations
SDG alignment is assessed from consumer discourse, not from verified programme outcomes
Gender (SDG 5) signals are particularly underrepresented in current data sources
Geographic demand signals
Indicative
Claude AIAll sources
Relative conversation volume by Nigerian state, inferred from geographic references within public content. Includes explicit state/city mentions, local landmark references, and LGA names. Scores are relative — Lagos at 100 means Lagos generates the most energy discourse, not an absolute conversation count.
Limitations
Many conversations contain no geographic reference — these are excluded from state mapping
Urban areas are heavily overrepresented relative to rural populations
High conversation volume ≠ high energy need — it reflects digital discourse, not energy poverty
Fossil vs clean signal
High
Claude AIAll sources
Proportion of energy conversations referencing fossil fuel products (generators, diesel, petrol, kerosene) vs clean energy products (solar, mini-grid, battery storage, clean cooking). Classified at conversation level by Claude. A declining fossil share over time is the key transition signal this metric tracks.
Limitations
Conversations may reference both fossil and clean products simultaneously
High fossil discourse may reflect complaints about fossil dependency rather than preference for it
Important limitation
Who Vitics data does not represent

Vitics data reflects the voices of Nigerians who are online, have smartphones, and participate in digital discourse. This is a significant and growing population — but it is not the full Nigerian population.

Who is underrepresented: Rural communities with limited internet connectivity, elderly populations, non-smartphone users, people in the deepest energy poverty, and populations who primarily communicate in languages other than English or Nigerian Pidgin.

What this means: Vitics insights describe digital consumer discourse about energy — not a representative survey of all Nigerian energy consumers. Policy makers and investors should treat Vitics as one intelligence input among several, complemented by field surveys, national statistics, and community-level research.

We believe being honest about this limitation makes Vitics more valuable, not less.
