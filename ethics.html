Data Ethics Statement
Ethics & Transparency
How Vitics handles
data responsibly
Vitics is a public-interest energy intelligence platform. We believe that meaningful insights about Nigeria's energy transition can be generated ethically, transparently, and without compromising the privacy of any individual. This page explains exactly how we do that.
Our purpose
What Vitics is — and what it is not

Vitics is an AI-powered decentralised energy intelligence platform that analyses publicly available consumer conversations to generate anonymised insights into Nigeria's energy transition. Our goal is to surface patterns, trends, and signals that help energy investors, policy makers, development researchers, and energy startups make better decisions.

Vitics is not a surveillance tool. We do not track individuals. We do not build user profiles. We do not sell personal data. We do not monitor specific people or organisations. Our unit of analysis is always the conversation, never the person.

This distinction — between consumer intelligence and user surveillance — is fundamental to everything we build and every decision we make.

Principles
Our five data ethics principles
Anonymisation by design
No individual post, username, profile, or identity is ever stored or displayed. Every insight is derived from aggregated patterns across thousands of conversations — never from a single identifiable source.
Data minimisation
We collect only what is necessary to generate energy intelligence. Video titles, comment themes, engagement patterns, and topic signals — not usernames, profile photos, device identifiers, or personal contact information.
Public interest framing
Vitics operates as a public-interest research platform. Our insights serve energy policy, development finance, and consumer welfare — not commercial advertising, political targeting, or individual profiling.
Transparency of method
Every insight on the Vitics dashboard carries a methodology indicator showing its data source, confidence level, and analytical basis. We do not publish black-box scores without explanation.
Vulnerable population sensitivity
Nigerian energy consumers include people experiencing energy poverty, rural communities, women-led micro-enterprises, and marginalised groups. Insights derived from their digital conversations are handled with care. We acknowledge that social media data skews toward urban, educated, and smartphone-owning populations — and we note this limitation explicitly in every relevant insight rather than presenting a partial picture as a complete one.
Data collection
What we collect — and what we never collect
What Vitics collects
Publicly visible post text and comment themes
Video titles, descriptions, and topic metadata
Engagement volume and trend signals
Product mentions and brand references
Geographic references made within public content
Hashtag and keyword frequency patterns
Aggregated sentiment signals across topic clusters
Public forum discussion themes and complaint patterns
What Vitics never collects
Usernames, handles, or display names
Profile photos or avatar images
Email addresses or phone numbers
Device identifiers or IP addresses
Data from private groups, DMs, or paywalled content
Content collected in violation of platform Terms of Service
Health, financial, or sensitive personal information
Location data beyond what users publicly state in content

Legal compliance
Our legal framework

Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) — As a platform generating insights about Nigerian consumers, Vitics complies fully with the NDPA enforced by the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC). Our data processing is grounded in legitimate public interest, with anonymisation applied before any storage or display of insights.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — Because Vitics serves European partners, researchers, and investors, we apply GDPR principles universally across the platform. This includes the right to erasure, data minimisation, purpose limitation, and privacy by design.

Platform Terms of Service — All data collection is conducted through official APIs or publicly accessible channels. We do not use automated scraping tools in violation of any platform's terms of service. Our data sourcing strategy is documented and reviewable.

Honest limitations
What our data cannot tell you

We believe intellectual honesty about data limitations is as important as the insights themselves. Every user of Vitics should understand the following:

Social media data skews toward connected populations. Our insights reflect the voices of Nigerians who are online, have smartphones, and participate in digital discourse. Rural, offline, and non-smartphone-owning populations — who may face the greatest energy access challenges — are underrepresented in our data.

Emissions estimates are modelled, not metered. Our CO₂ avoided calculations are estimates derived from conversion factors and adoption proxies — not direct measurements from energy meters. We indicate this clearly on every emissions insight.

Sentiment analysis is probabilistic. AI-generated sentiment scores carry inherent uncertainty. Nigerian English, Pidgin, Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo linguistic patterns require specific model tuning. We report confidence levels alongside every sentiment score.

Geographic inference is approximate. Location signals are extracted from text references within public content. They are indicative of discourse geography, not precise population distribution.

Our commitment
Vitics is built to serve Nigeria's energy transition — not to exploit it
Every architectural decision, every data pipeline, and every insight displayed on this platform has been made with the question: does this serve the public interest without harming any individual? We will continue updating this statement as the platform evolves, as new data sources are added, and as the legal and ethical landscape around AI and data develops. We welcome feedback, challenge, and scrutiny from researchers, regulators, civil society, and the public.
